Basic Logic Gates

Welcome to the first lesson on logic gates and circuits. This lesson will go over the basic elements of logic gates and how they are used in circuits. In this lesson we will cover:

  • What a logic gate is
  • How logic gates are used
  • Basic types of logic gates (AND, OR and NOT)

What is a logic gate?

Logic gates, also known as logic circuits, are the elements of a circuit that take binary input and produce binary output, depending upon the type of gate and the inputs recieved. As you can imagine this is extremely useful and logic gates form a fundamental part of modern computers. The complicated circuitry that allow computers to quickly perform arithmetic are built upon these relatively simple building blocks.

How are they used?

As mentioned above, logic gates work by accepting binary output and outputting either a 1 or a 0 depending upon whether or not the inputs matched the conditions specified by the particular circuit they were being passed through.

AND Gate

The AND gate is one of the most simple but important gates. The AND gate takes two inputs and returns:
